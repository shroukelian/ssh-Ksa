Establishing Business in Saudi Arabia: Your Comprehensive Guide in the Era of Vision 2030

By: SSH Strategic Team March 2025

The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia holds an advanced position in the global Ease of Doing Business index, thanks to the fundamental reforms launched by Vision 2030 to diversify the economy and attract investment. Establishing a business in the Kingdom today is faster and simpler than ever before, with continuous golden investment opportunities in various sectors such as tourism, entertainment, technology, industry, and healthcare.

1. Why Choose Saudi Arabia to Establish Your Business?

  • Massive Economy: The largest economy in the Arab world with a GDP exceeding $1 trillion.
  • Huge Consumer Market: More than 35 million people with high purchasing power.
  • World-Class Infrastructure: Advanced ports, roads, and telecommunication networks supporting logistics.
  • Investment Incentives: Tax exemptions, grants, and government support for both local and foreign investors.
  • 100% Foreign Ownership: The possibility for foreigners to own 100% of the company in many sectors.

SSH Tip:

Before starting, study the requirements of your specific sector carefully; some activities require special licenses from specialized authorities or specific conditions for foreign investors.

2. Types of Legal Entities Available for Formation

A. Limited Liability Company (LLC): The most requested type for small and medium enterprises, characterized by flexibility in the number of partners.

B. Joint Stock Company (JSC): Suitable for large-scale projects requiring capital starting from SAR 500,000 and allowing for public offering.

C. Foreign Branch: Allows international companies to operate in Saudi Arabia without establishing a completely independent legal entity.

3. Steps to Establish a Company in Saudi Arabia

  1. Market Study: Ensuring the feasibility of the activity and determining the geographic location.
  2. Trade Name Reservation: Done through the Ministry of Commerce electronic platform.
  3. Drafting Articles of Association: Defining capital, shares, and management mechanisms.
  4. Commercial & Tax Registration: Obtaining the Commercial Registration (CR) and Tax Number from ZATCA.
  5. Government Platforms: Registering in Mudad, Qiwa, and Social Insurance (GOSI).

Strategic Advice:

Engaging a legal and accounting consultant during the "Articles of Association" drafting phase guarantees your rights and helps you avoid legal loopholes that could be costly in the future.

4. Requirements for Establishing Foreign Companies

For foreign investors, an investment license must be obtained from the Ministry of Investment (MISA). All parent company documents must be attested, and there must be a commitment to the required Saudization percentages in the (Nitaqat) program.
