Financial Funding Advisory: Your Key to Securing Capital for Business Growth

By: SSH Strategic Team March 28, 2025

Funding is the lifeblood of any project or company. In the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, with the expansion of the investment environment and the diversity of available funding tools, identifying the optimal source of funding and negotiating to obtain it on the best terms has become a real challenge for entrepreneurs and business owners. This is where specialized funding advisory services step in to turn this challenge into an opportunity.

1. What are Financial Funding Advisory Services?

Funding advisory is a specialized service aimed at:

  • Assessing the actual funding needs of the company or project.
  • Identifying the most suitable sources of available funding.
  • Preparing professional funding files and required documents.
  • Negotiating with financiers to obtain the best terms.
  • Structuring funding in a way that maintains company sustainability and growth.

SSH Tip:

Many companies fail to obtain proper funding not because their projects aren't serious, but because their funding files are not properly prepared to convince financiers.

2. Types of Funding Sources in Saudi Arabia

A. Traditional and Islamic Bank Financing

Saudi banks (SNB, Al Rajhi, Riyad, Inma, etc.) provide diverse financing products including operational financing for working capital, asset financing for equipment, project financing, and commercial real estate financing.

B. Investment Funds and Venture Capital (VC)

There is an increasing number of VC funds and early-stage investment in the Kingdom, especially in the tech and startup sectors, led by the Public Investment Fund (PIF) and its subsidiaries, alongside SRSB and others.

C. Government Funding Programs

The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia provides generous financial support through several channels:

  • Social Development Bank: Provides soft loans for small projects.
  • Saudi EXIM Bank: Specific support for Saudi exporters.
  • Kafalah: An SME financing guarantee program.
  • Monsha'at: Supporting and developing startups and SMEs through various initiatives.

D. Crowdfunding

Crowdfunding platforms approved by the Capital Market Authority (CMA), such as Scopeer and Manafa, provide a financing alternative for projects that attract a wide audience.

E. Initial Public Offering (IPO) and Private Equity

For mature companies, listing shares on the Saudi Stock Exchange (Tadawul) allows for raising massive capital to fund expansion.

Statistic: Saudi SMEs received financing exceeding SAR 45 billion in 2023 through government and private channels, with this figure expected to double by 2030.

3. Steps to Obtain the Right Funding

  1. Identify Needs: Precisely determine how much you need, for what purpose, and what repayment schedule you can sustain.
  2. Prepare the Funding File: Includes a detailed Business Plan, Feasibility Study, Financial Models, and audited financial statements.
  3. Connect with Financiers: Choose entities that align most with your project type, size, and development stage.
  4. Negotiate Terms: Professional negotiation on interest or profit rates, repayment periods, and collateral can save you hundreds of thousands of riyals.

4. SSH Services in Funding Advisory

  • Evaluating funding position and current financial status to identify the best options.
  • Preparing professional funding files based on international standards.
  • Connecting with financiers through our extensive relationship network.
  • Negotiating terms to represent your company's interests effectively.
  • Managing follow-up until funds are released.

Important Alert:

Do not enter negotiations without a pre-prepared professional file. First impressions with financiers are irreplaceable, and a weak file can close doors even if your project is promising.

5. Common Mistakes in Funding Requests

Mistakes include requesting too much or too little, failing to prepare realistic financial forecasts, relying on a single financier, overlooking collateral requirements, and ignoring funding costs in feasibility calculations.
