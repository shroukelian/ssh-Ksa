Your Guide to Filing Error-Free Tax Returns

By: SSH Strategic Team March 10, 2025

Tax returns represent one of the most important financial obligations for any company, whether small or large. With the development of tax systems in the Gulf – especially in Saudi Arabia, Bahrain, and the UAE – accuracy and timely filing have become indispensable to ensure business stability and growth.

In this article, we outline practical steps for filing an error-free tax return, with tips from the SSH team based on real-world practice.

1. Ensure Your Documents Are Organized First

The most common error is starting the return preparation without pre-organizing data and invoices. To ensure accuracy, the following should be organized:

  • Detailed sales and purchase invoices.
  • Updated bank statements.
  • Tax receipts (if any).
  • Documents for legal tax deductions and exemptions.

SSH Tip:

Use a digital archiving system (Cloud Storage) that makes it easy to reference later when audits are requested by official authorities.

2. Review Value Added Tax (VAT) Classifications

Some sales may be zero-rated or completely exempt, and errors in this classification lead to submitting incorrect financial data that may expose you to liability.

  • Verify if products/services are subject to the standard, zero, or exempt rate.
  • Ensure correct tax classification of suppliers (local/international).

Expert Tip:

Do not rely on intuition for classification; review every item with an accounting expert familiar with the latest local legislation in the Kingdom.

3. Use Certified and Tax-Compliant Accounting Software

Relying on manual filing or traditional Excel files increases the likelihood of human error. Certified programs provide you with:

  • Automatic and accurate VAT report generation.
  • Full support for Arabic and English languages.
  • The ability to link directly with tax authority platforms (such as the Fatoora platform).

4. Do Not Delay Filing Until the Last Moment

Every delay in submission can lead to heavy financial fines or even the freezing of the facility's tax number, which disrupts business operations.

Organizational Tip:

Set an internal tax calendar for your company that ends at least two weeks before the official deadline to ensure there is time for review.

5. Review the Return More Than Once Before Submission

Final review is the fine line between acceptance and audit. Ensure the consistency of:

  • The correct Tax Registration Number.
  • Matching accounting periods with the entered data.
  • Highly accurate standard and exempt amounts.
  • Carried-forward balances from previous tax periods.

SSH Tip:

Always ask another accountant or an external consulting firm to conduct an independent Peer Review before hitting the submit button.

6. Keep Proof of Submission and Tax Archives

Proof of submission is as important as the return itself, especially in cases of objection or surprise tax audits. Keep a copy of the sent return, the receipt notice, and all attachments sent to the authority.

Conclusion: Accurate tax returns mean peace of mind, avoiding fines, and a respectable financial reputation before official authorities. Do not treat the return as a routine procedure, but as an opportunity to prove your professional commitment and the strength of your financial system.
