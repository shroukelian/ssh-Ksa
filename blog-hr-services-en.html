HR Services in Saudi Arabia: Your Guide to Full Compliance with Labor Laws and Saudization

By: SSH Strategic Team March 25, 2025

Human Resources management in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia involves more than just recruitment and payroll. It encompasses a complete system of governmental and regulatory requirements that are constantly evolving—from Labor Law and Nitaqat (Saudization) to updates in the Social Insurance system and the Wage Protection System. Proper compliance with these requirements ensures your company avoids fines and fosters a positive, productive work environment.

1. Essential Government Requirements for HR Management

A. Registration with General Organization for Social Insurance (GOSI)

Every company is obligated to register its employees with GOSI immediately upon hiring. Key details include:

  • Saudi Employee Contribution: 10% of the salary (9% for pension + 1% for occupational hazards).
  • Employer Contribution: 12% for Saudi employees (11% pension + 1% occupational hazards).
  • Non-Saudi Employee: 2% paid by the employer only (occupational hazards).
  • Payment Deadline: Before the 15th of each calendar month.

B. Wage Protection System (WPS) - Mudad Platform

All companies are required to process their employees' salaries through the "Mudad" platform to ensure transparency and protect workers' rights:

  • Monthly update of payroll data on the platform.
  • Ensuring salary disbursement on specified dates.
  • Non-compliance exposes the company to the freezing of its Commercial Registration.

C. Work Visas and Residency (Iqama)

Recruiting foreign employees requires precise procedures including: applying for recruitment permits via the Musaned platform, meeting Nitaqat Saudization requirements, and renewing residencies on time to avoid violations.

Warning:

Violations of the Labor Law in Saudi Arabia can lead to fines of up to SAR 100,000, freezing of recruitment files, and suspension of government services.

2. The Saudization Program (Nitaqat)

The Nitaqat program classifies companies into categories (Platinum, High Green, Mid Green, Low Green, Yellow, Red) based on the percentage of Saudi employees. Your category directly impacts recruitment capabilities, work permit renewals, access to Ministry of HR services, and participation in government tenders.

How to raise your Saudization percentage? Through cooperation with youth welfare authorities, utilizing wage support programs like "Hadaf" (HRDF), and developing an attractive work environment for Saudi talent.

SSH Tip:

Reaching the "Green" or "Platinum" category in Nitaqat opens wide possibilities for growing your business without recruitment restrictions.

3. Drafting Compliant Employment Contracts

The Saudi Labor Law specifies minimum requirements in an employment contract. A robust contract includes: a clear and detailed job description, salary components (basic + allowances), contract duration and renewal terms, probation period conditions, working hours, leave policies, confidentiality/non-compete clauses, and termination procedures with severance pay.

4. Performance and Professional Development Systems

An effective performance management system contributes to motivating employees, identifying training needs, documenting performance for promotion or termination decisions, and complying with regulatory requirements regarding workforce development.

5. SSH Human Resources Management Services

  • GOSI Registration: Completing employee registration and contribution calculations.
  • Payroll Preparation: Accurate processing of monthly salaries via the Mudad platform.
  • Work Permit Management: Following up on residency and work permit renewals.
  • Nitaqat Consulting: Assessing your status and developing a plan to improve your category.
  • Contract Drafting: Preparing contracts compliant with the Saudi Labor Law.
  • Labor Dispute Representation: Defending your company's interests in labor courts.

SSH Tip:

Establishing an organized and compliant HR department from the start saves your company subsequent correction costs and builds a reliable reputation as an employer.

6. Key HR Challenges in Saudi Arabia

  • Talent Retention: Necessity of attractive HR policies (competitive salaries, positive environment).
  • Digital Transformation: Relying on digital HRMS systems to improve operational efficiency.
  • Business Needs vs. Saudization: Balancing recruitment of required talent with Saudization quotas.

7. Government HR Support Programs

  • Hadaf (HRDF): Supporting the training and employment of Saudis by compensating part of their salaries.
  • Saned: Financial support for unemployed Saudi youth.
  • Taqat: The national employment portal for searching for Saudi talent.
  • Nafis: A program to develop Saudi human capital.
