HR Services in Saudi Arabia: Your Guide to Full Compliance with Labor Laws and Saudization

By: SSH Strategic Team March 25, 2025

HR management in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia goes beyond simple hiring and payroll. It involves a comprehensive ecosystem of governmental and regulatory requirements that are constantly evolving, from Labor Law and Nitaqat (Saudization) to updates in the Social Insurance system and the Wage Protection System (WPS). Correct compliance ensures your company avoids fines and fosters a productive work environment.

1. Core Governmental Requirements for HR Management

A. Registration with Social Insurance (GOSI)

  • Saudi Employee Contribution: 10% of the salary.
  • Employer Contribution: 12% for Saudi employees and 2% for non-Saudis (Occupational Hazards).
  • Payment Deadline: Before the 15th of each calendar month.

B. Wage Protection System (WPS) - Mudad Platform

Companies are required to process payroll through the "Mudad" platform to ensure transparency. Non-compliance exposes the company to immediate freezing of its commercial registration.

Regulatory Warning:

Violations of the Labor Law in Saudi Arabia can lead to fines up to SAR 100,000, freezing of recruitment files, and complete suspension of government services.

2. The Saudization Program (Nitaqat)

The Nitaqat program classifies companies into categories (Platinum, Green, Yellow, Red) based on the percentage of Saudi employees. The category directly affects your ability to recruit from abroad and renew work permits.

SSH Tip:

Reaching the "Green" or "Platinum" category opens vast opportunities for business growth without recruitment restrictions or sponsorship transfer hurdles.

3. Drafting Compliant Employment Contracts

A robust employment contract is the company's safety valve. It must include a clear job description, detailed salary and allowance breakdown, contract duration, working hours, and confidentiality and non-compete clauses according to the new Labor Law.

4. SSH HR Management Services

  • GOSI Registration: Managing employee registration and accurate contribution calculations.
  • Payroll Processing: Handling monthly payroll cycles through the Mudad platform.
  • Nitaqat Consulting: Evaluating your current status and creating a plan to improve your category.
  • Legal Representation: Defending your company's interests in labor court disputes.

5. Government HR Support Programs

  • Hadaf (HRDF): Supporting training and hiring of Saudis by compensating a portion of their salaries.
  • Taqat: The national employment portal to find qualified Saudi talent.
  • Nafis: A program dedicated to developing Saudi human capital.
