Your Guide to Error-Free Tax Returns in Saudi Arabia

By: SSH Strategic Team March 10, 2025

Filing tax returns in Saudi Arabia, whether for Value Added Tax (VAT), Corporate Tax, or Zakat, requires high precision to avoid penalties from the Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority (ZATCA). This guide provides clear steps for error-free submission, emphasizing the importance of specialist review to ensure compliance.

1. Understanding Tax Types in Saudi Arabia

  • Value Added Tax (VAT): 15% (since July 2020) on most goods and services.
  • Corporate Tax: 20% on the profits of non-Saudi or mixed companies.
  • Business Zakat: 2.5% on the net wealth of Saudi-owned companies.
  • Registration: Mandatory for companies with annual revenues exceeding SAR 375,000.

SSH Tip:

Regularly visit the official ZATCA website (www.zatca.gov.sa) to understand the latest regulatory updates for full compliance.

2. Organizing Financial Records

Accurate financial records are the foundation of a successful return. Ensure the availability of:

  • E-Invoices: Mandatory through the "Fatoora" system since December 2021.
  • Accounting Records: Ledger, balance sheet, and income statements.
  • Bank Statements: To track all payments and flows.
  • Deductible Expenses: Such as taxable purchases.

Technical Tip:

Use accounting software compatible with the "Fatoora" system, such as (Daftra, Odoo, Qoyod), to facilitate electronic integration.

3. Compliance with Saudi Tax Laws

  • Filing Deadlines: Quarterly for VAT (before the end of the month following the tax period).
  • Exemptions: Educational services, healthcare, and exports are exempt or zero-rated.
  • Late Fines: Ranging from 5% to 25% of the tax due.

Warning:

Late filing fines in the Kingdom can be significant; therefore, a specialist review is a security necessity for your business.

4. Filing through the ZATCA Portal

The Kingdom adopts the ZATCA electronic portal as a unified platform. Steps include: Logging in, entering sales and purchase data (Excel import available), using the validation tool, and finally retaining the confirmation notice.

5. Reviewing the Return Before Submission

We always recommend double-checking through specialists or auditing software, ensuring exact matching between physical records and digital return data, and verifying Input and Output tax balances.

Timing Tip:

Dedicate at least two days for a final review with your financial advisor to avoid any unintended human errors.

6. Importance of Consulting Accounting Specialists

Enlisting the help of legal accounting and tax consulting specialists helps ensure full compliance with ZATCA laws, maximizes the benefit of legal exemptions, and significantly reduces financial risks.

7. Record and Document Retention

According to regulations, tax returns, invoices, and financial documents must be kept for at least 5 years to be ready for any potential audits.

Archiving Tip:

Use secure cloud storage systems like OneDrive or Google Drive to prevent document loss and ensure easy access.

8. Avoiding Common Errors

Common errors include missing submission deadlines, e-invoicing errors, and failing to update company information (such as address changes) in a timely manner.
