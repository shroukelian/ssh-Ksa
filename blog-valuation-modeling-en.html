Valuation and Financial Modeling Services: The Scientific Foundation for Your Investment Decisions

By: SSH Strategic Team March 18, 2025

In the competitive business world that the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia is witnessing as part of the Vision 2030 journey, investment and operational decisions are no longer based on intuition or approximate estimates. Today, successful companies rely on accurate analytical financial models and objective valuations of their assets and businesses to make strategic decisions based on data and facts.

1. What are Financial Valuation Services?

Financial valuation is the process of determining the fair value of companies, assets, or projects based on approved accounting and statistical standards and methodologies. It is used in:

  • Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A).
  • Initial Public Offerings (IPO).
  • Selling assets or companies.
  • Project financing and obtaining bank loans.
  • Litigation and commercial disputes.
  • Financial restructuring.
  • Strategic planning and capital allocation.

SSH Tip:

Accurate valuation maximizes the value of your deals and protects your rights in any negotiation with investors, buyers, or financing entities.

2. Approved Financial Valuation Methodologies

A. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Method: Considered the most accurate and common in corporate valuation, as it is based on forecasting future cash flows for the company or project and discounting these flows at a discount rate that reflects the cost of capital and risks to arrive at the Net Present Value of the business.

B. Market Multiples Method: Compares the company with similar companies listed on the stock exchange or similar deals, using indicators such as Price-to-Earnings (P/E Ratio), EBITDA multiple, and Sales multiple (EV/Revenue).

C. Net Asset Value Method: Valued based on the fair market value of the company's assets minus its liabilities, primarily used in evaluating real estate and holding companies.

D. Comparable Transactions Method: Analyzing similar acquisition and merger deals in the market to arrive at benchmark pricing standards.

Technical Tip:

In most cases, more than one methodology is used to reach a reliable valuation range, rather than a single number.

3. What are Financial Analytical Models?

Financial Models are analytical tools built in software like Excel or Python to simulate the future financial performance of the company. They include:

  • A. Financial Forecast Model: Guides management in annual and medium-term planning, including projections for sales, expenses, and cash flow.
  • B. Sensitivity Analysis Model: Measures the impact of changes in key variables (such as selling prices or production costs) on financial performance.
  • C. Investment Valuation Model: Evaluates the feasibility of proposed investments through indicators such as Net Present Value (NPV), Internal Rate of Return (IRR), and Payback Period.
  • D. Capital Structure Model: Analyzes the optimal balance between debt financing and equity financing to achieve the lowest Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC).

4. Use Cases for Valuation and Analytical Models

A. Before Acquisition or Merger: Determining a fair price for the target company and evaluating the expected strategic benefits from the deal.

B. To Attract Investors: Providing professional financial models and reliable valuations that convince investors of your project's feasibility and strengthen your negotiating position.

C. To Obtain Bank Financing: Banks require detailed financial models and valuations of collateral before granting any financing.

D. To Make Expansion Decisions: Analyzing the feasibility of entering new markets or developing new products based on accurate financial models.

Statistic: Studies indicate that companies that rely on advanced financial models in their decisions achieve investment returns 25-30% higher compared to companies that make their decisions intuitively.

The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia adopts International Valuation Standards (IVS) and Capital Market Authority (CMA) requirements for valuations provided in official contexts such as IPOs and mergers.
