Valuation and Financial Modeling Services: The Scientific Foundation for Your Investment Decisions

By: SSH Strategic Team March 18, 2025

In the competitive business world that Saudi Arabia is witnessing within the journey of Vision 2030, investment and operational decisions are no longer based on intuition or approximate estimates. Today, successful companies rely on accurate analytical financial models and objective valuations of their assets and businesses to make strategic decisions based on data and facts.

1. What are Financial Valuation Services?

Financial valuation is the process of determining the fair value of companies, assets, or projects based on approved accounting and statistical standards and methodologies. It is used in:

  • Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A).
  • Initial Public Offerings (IPO).
  • Selling assets or companies.
  • Project financing and obtaining bank loans.
  • Litigation and commercial disputes.
  • Financial restructuring and strategic planning.

SSH Tip:

Accurate valuation maximizes the value of your deals and protects your rights in any negotiations with investors, buyers, or financing entities.

2. Approved Financial Valuation Methodologies

A. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF): The most accurate method, based on forecasting future cash flows and discounting them at a rate that reflects risks and the cost of capital.

B. Market Multiples: Comparing the entity with similar companies using indicators such as the Price-to-Earnings (P/E) ratio or EBITDA multiples.

C. Net Asset Value: Evaluated based on the fair market value of the company's assets minus its liabilities; mostly used for real estate companies.

D. Comparable Transactions: Analyzing similar acquisition and merger deals in the market to reach benchmark pricing.

Technical Tip:

In most professional cases, more than one methodology is used to reach a reliable Valuation Range, rather than just a single number.

3. What are Financial Analytical Models?

Financial Models are analytical tools built to simulate the company's future financial performance, and they include:

  • Budget and Forecast Model: Guides management in annual planning and cash flow projection.
  • Sensitivity Analysis Model: Measures the impact of changes in key variables (such as prices or costs) on profits.
  • Investment Valuation Model: Measures feasibility through indicators like NPV, IRR, and payback period.
  • Capital Structure Model: Analyzes the optimal balance between debt and equity to achieve the lowest Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC).

4. Use Cases for Valuation and Analytical Models

These models are essential before an acquisition or merger to determine a fair price, to attract investors with reliable data, to obtain bank financing where banks require detailed collateral valuations, and finally to make decisions on expansion and entering new markets.

Important Statistic: Studies indicate that companies that adopt advanced financial models in their decisions achieve investment returns 25-30% higher compared to companies that make decisions intuitively.

5. SSH Services in Valuation and Modeling

The specialized SSH team offers valuation services for companies and projects, valuation of intangible assets (trademarks and patents), building customized financial models, and feasibility analysis to support Board of Director decisions.

6. Approved Valuation Standards in Saudi Arabia

The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia adopts International Valuation Standards (IVS) and Capital Market Authority (CMA) requirements for valuations submitted in official contexts such as IPOs and mergers.
