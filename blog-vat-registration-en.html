VAT Registration in Saudi Arabia: Your Comprehensive Guide to Tax Compliance

By: SSH Strategic Team March 15, 2025

Since the implementation of Value Added Tax (VAT) in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia in January 2018, and its subsequent increase to 15% in July 2020, proper registration and continuous compliance with the Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority (ZATCA) requirements have become a top priority for every company operating in the Saudi market. Failure to register or comply can expose your company to heavy financial fines and legal consequences.

1. What is Value Added Tax (VAT) in Saudi Arabia?

VAT is an indirect tax imposed on the consumption of goods and services at each stage of the production and distribution chain. In Saudi Arabia:

  • Current Rate: 15% on most goods and services (since July 2020).
  • Exemptions: Healthcare services, education, exports, and specific financial services.
  • Regulatory Authority: Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority (ZATCA).
  • Digital Portal: ZATCA electronic platform (zatca.gov.sa).

SSH Tip:

Even if your sales are below the mandatory threshold, voluntary registration may be beneficial to recover input tax and improve your financial standing.

2. Who is Obligated to Register for VAT?

A. Mandatory Registration: You must register if your annual taxable sales exceed SAR 375,000 over the past 12 months, or if you expect to exceed this limit within the next 30 days.

B. Voluntary Registration: Available for businesses with annual sales between SAR 187,500 and SAR 375,000 to enable them to claim input tax credits.

C. Non-resident Businesses: Foreign entities providing goods or services to customers in the Kingdom are obligated to register for VAT regardless of their sales volume.

Warning:

Failing to register after reaching the mandatory threshold can lead to an immediate fine of up to SAR 10,000.

3. Steps for VAT Registration

The registration process is handled electronically through the ZATCA portal and involves the following steps:

  1. Document Preparation: Valid Commercial Registration (CR), National ID of the owner/partners, bank statements (last 12 months), and revenue data.
  2. Online Application: Log in to the ZATCA portal, complete the VAT registration form, and upload the required documents.
  3. Receiving TRN: Once approved, you will receive a 15-digit Tax Registration Number (TRN) which must be displayed on all invoices and official documents.

4. Periodic Obligations After Registration

  • Filing Returns: Monthly (for businesses with sales > SAR 40M) or Quarterly (for sales < SAR 40M).
  • E-Invoicing System: Adherence to the "Fatoora" system is mandatory, including integration with ZATCA during Phase 2.
  • Retention: All invoices and financial records must be safely archived for at least 5 years.

5. Common VAT Fines to Avoid

  • Failure to register on time: Up to SAR 10,000.
  • Late filing of returns: 5% to 25% of the tax due.
  • Errors in tax returns: 50% of the tax difference.
  • Failure to issue correct invoices: Up to SAR 50,000.
  • Failure to maintain records: Up to SAR 50,000.
