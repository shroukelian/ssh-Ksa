VAT Registration in Saudi Arabia: Your Comprehensive Guide to Tax Compliance

By: SSH Strategic Team March 15, 2025

Since the implementation of Value Added Tax (VAT) in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia in January 2018, and its increase to 15% in July 2020, correct registration and continuous compliance with the Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority (ZATCA) requirements have become top priorities for every company operating in the Saudi market. Negligence in registration or compliance may expose your company to burdensome financial fines and legal procedures.

1. What is Value Added Tax (VAT) in Saudi Arabia?

Value Added Tax (VAT) is an indirect tax imposed on the consumption of goods and services at various stages of production and distribution. In the Kingdom:

  • Current Rate: 15% on most goods and services (since July 2020).
  • Exempt Goods: Healthcare services, education, exports, and certain financial services.
  • Supervisory Authority: Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority (ZATCA).
  • Registration & Returns Portal: ZATCA electronic platform.

SSH Tip:

Even if your sales are below the mandatory threshold, voluntary registration may be beneficial in some cases to recover input tax and strengthen your financial position.

2. Who is Obligated to Register for VAT?

A. Mandatory Registration: You must register if your total taxable sales exceed SAR 375,000 over the past 12 months, or if you expect to exceed this threshold within the next 30 days.

B. Voluntary Registration: Available for those whose annual sales range between SAR 187,500 and SAR 375,000, enabling them to recover input tax.

C. Non-resident Companies: Foreign companies providing services or goods to customers within the Kingdom are obligated to register for VAT regardless of sales volume.

Warning:

Delaying registration after reaching the mandatory threshold may expose you to an immediate fine of up to SAR 10,000.

3. Steps for VAT Registration

The registration process is conducted electronically via the ZATCA portal and requires the following steps:

  1. Prepare Documents: Valid Commercial Registration (CR), National ID of the owner/partners, bank statements for the last 12 months, and revenue data.
  2. Digital Registration: Access the (zatca.gov.sa) platform and accurately complete the VAT registration form while uploading the required documents.
  3. Obtain TRN: Upon approval, you will receive a 15-digit Tax Registration Number (TRN) that must be included in all your electronic invoices and official documents.

4. Periodic Obligations After Registration

  • Filing Returns: Monthly for large enterprises (annual sales exceeding SAR 40 million) and quarterly for others.
  • Filing Deadline: During the month following the end of the tax period.
  • E-Invoicing System: Obligatory use of the "Fatoora" system to issue approved electronic invoices and link with ZATCA in the second phase.
  • Archiving: Retaining all invoices and records (issued and received) for at least 5 years.

5. Common VAT Penalties to Avoid

  • Failure to register on time: Fine up to SAR 10,000.
  • Late filing of tax returns: Fine of 5-25% of the tax due.
  • Errors in the return: Fine of 50% of the tax difference.
  • Failure to issue correct invoices: Fine up to SAR 50,000.
  • Failure to maintain records: Fine up to SAR 50,000.
