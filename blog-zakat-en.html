Zakat Services for Companies in Saudi Arabia: Your Guide to Full Compliance with ZATCA

By: SSH Strategic Team March 12, 2025

Zakat is a pillar of Islam and both a religious and legal obligation for Saudi companies and businesses owned by Saudi nationals. The Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority (ZATCA) oversees the collection of Zakat from companies in accordance with Islamic Sharia provisions and specific accounting mechanisms. Preparing accurate Zakat returns and complying with requirements requires specialized expertise that combines Islamic jurisprudence with forensic accounting knowledge.

1. Who is Subject to Zakat Among Companies?

  • Fully Saudi-owned Companies: Companies 100% owned by Saudi nationals.
  • Mixed Companies: Zakat is calculated only on the Saudi partners' share of profits and wealth.
  • Saudi Individual Partners: In limited liability companies and general partnerships.

Alert:

Foreign companies fully owned by non-Saudis are not subject to Zakat, but rather to Income Tax at a rate of 20%.

2. How is Corporate Zakat Calculated?

The Sharia rate for Zakat is 2.5% of the Zakat base. ZATCA adopts one of the following two methods:

  • Net Assets Method: Calculated through: Equity + Long-term Liabilities - Fixed Assets - Long-term Investments.
  • Adjusted Net Income Method: Calculated based on accounting net income with specific standard adjustments made by ZATCA.

SSH Tip:

Choosing the appropriate method to calculate the Zakat base can significantly affect the amount due. The SSH team helps you choose the optimal method according to your company's circumstances.

3. Deadlines and Procedures for Filing Zakat Returns

The Zakat return must be submitted within 120 days of the end of the company's fiscal year. Delay in submission exposes your company to a late filing fine of up to 25% of the Zakat due.

4. Common Challenges in Zakat Calculation

Companies face challenges such as complex corporate structures (holding and subsidiary companies) and distinguishing between expenses recognized and not recognized by ZATCA.

5. Zakat Services Provided by SSH

  • Preparation and submission of Zakat returns with professional accuracy.
  • Settlements and appeals against ZATCA assessments.
  • Advance Zakat planning to improve the entity's financial position.

6. Difference Between Zakat and Income Tax in KSA

Zakat (2.5%) applies to Saudi shares, while Income Tax (20%) applies to foreign shares. Mixed companies are subject to both based on ownership percentages.
